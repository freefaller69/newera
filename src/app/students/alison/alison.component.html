Alison Sienkewicz

She composes and reads email and checks the paper regularly for movies and musical events that she enjoys.

Alison was born April 6, 1970. I’ll never forget the proclamation the obstetrician made when she was born. Your baby is a mongoloid Joanie, and you’ll have to put her in an institution, but there are no good ones in Washington State. For many years afterward, I was invited to speak to medical students about how it felt to deliver a child you weren’t expecting and what was the best thing to say to parents!

Before going to the EEU Alison was involved in an infant stimulation project with Dr. Howenstein at Fircrest School. This was a good foundation for learning behavior modification techniques.

We felt extremely fortunate to be involved in the model preschool program with Teacher Pat, Val and all the energetic and supportive graduate students in special education. It was an exciting time to be in such a progressive program. And it gave us the confidence that Alison had the capability to succeed at far greater things than we might have expected.

Alison went on to graduate from high school. She worked for several years at Pizza Hut and at the Faculty Club at the UW. Recently she decided to retire after seeing so many friends and family do the same. Life for her now involves doing individual sports like golf and tennis (she was a Down Syndrome Community calendar girl), travelling, cooking, and keeping house with her three housemates. She composes and reads email and checks the paper regularly for movies and musical events that she enjoys. She loves dogs, dancing and country music.

Joan (not Joanie) Sienkiewicz
